I still remember building my first website in HTML back in 5th grade—the thrill of seeing my code come to life on screen ignited something in me. Today, I am proud to say that I have officially graduated from Oregon State University with a B.S. in Business Analytics, receiving the university's prestigious Award for Outstanding Achievement, an honor reserved for just the top 3 graduating students in the major this year.

I chose business analytics because it perfectly merges my core technical capabilities with my fascination for understanding consumer behavior and conversion metrics. I thrive at the intersection of data engineering, funnel optimization, and high-growth strategy—building backend pipelines, automating infrastructure, and translating complex mathematical data models into visual business intelligence tools.

Throughout my academic journey, I have focused on launching high-impact technical initiatives. I had the privilege of serving as the President of the OSU Business Analytics Club and acting as the Chief Marketing Officer for BeaverHacks, where I spearheaded digital optimization campaigns that generated 300,000+ views and scaled the event into Oregon's largest hackathon with over 450 attendees. Simultaneously, I applied my analytics toolkit within corporate and university structures, tracking metrics across $50M+ in digital assets at IndexCoop and architecting data scripts for 80,000+ enterprise users within university IT operations.

When I'm not running predictive models, text mining large-scale open-source datasets, or debugging relational databases, I am actively constructing full-stack systems alongside a passionate community of driven developers.
